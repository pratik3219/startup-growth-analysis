The Startup Growth Analytics project aims to uncover patterns and success factors that drive the growth of startups in India. Using a dataset of 125 startups from various sectors such as Finance, Technology, Education, Health-Tech, and Services, this project analyzes multiple dimensions including funding amount, funding rounds, number of investors, employees, market valuation, and location.

The analysis was conducted using Tableau, a powerful data visualization tool, to build an interactive dashboard that helps visualize relationships between funding, sector, city, and startup performance. Key visual elements include bar charts, bubble charts, and geographic maps that allow users to compare funding distribution, identify top-performing cities, and evaluate sector-wise investment trends.

The project follows a structured, data-driven approach:

Data Cleaning & Preparation - Removed inconsistencies such as currency symbols and converted funding values to numeric format.

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) - Explored relationships between city, industry, startup age, and success metrics.

Dashboard Creation - Designed an interactive Tableau dashboard to visualize patterns through funding trends, top sectors, and regional insights.

Insights & Storytelling - Identified key takeaways on how factors like location, sector, employee strength, and funding rounds influence startup success.

Findings reveal that Bengaluru dominates as the startup capital of India, attracting the highest funding across sectors. FinTech and Tech startups receive the largest investments and demonstrate the strongest growth potential. Moreover, startups with more experience, larger teams, and multiple funding rounds tend to have higher market valuations, suggesting that scalability and consistency are vital to long-term success.

Overall, the project provides valuable guidance for emerging entrepreneurs and investors, highlighting the strategic importance of location, funding diversity, and team development in building successful startups.
